Tier 1 · Hyperscale Production · Category 8

Anduril Arsenal-1

Pickaway County · Rickenbacker-adjacent

Arsenal-1 introduced a new category to the framework. Hyperscale autonomous-weapons manufacturing does not fit the SCIF, intelligence-facility or contractor-office signatures the original framework codified. The site announces itself — five million square feet cannot hide — but the secondary signals follow patterns the original taxonomy did not capture. Category 8 was added to read sites of this profile.

Building 1 (775,000 SF production plus 120,000 SF support) is well underway. Building 2 (924,000 SF) is raising walls. First YFQ-44A autonomous fighter production began Q1 2026. The product roadmap moves from Fury to Bolt drones, TITAN ground sensors with Palantir, and explicitly classified emerging technology. This is not a single program. It is a software-defined manufacturing platform housing many.

Tier 1 · Intelligence Production

NASIC Intelligence Production Complex III

Wright-Patterson AFB · Greene County

$156M, 255,000 SF five-story SCIF facility ribbon-cut May 2024. The complex consolidated National Air and Space Intelligence Center capacity at the highest classification levels — TEMPEST shielding, redundant electrical and mechanical systems, CBR-filtered HVAC, classified tier-2 data laboratories, vault doors. Personnel base now north of 3,300 with a $450M annual budget.

The Springfield 178th Wing intelligence mission feeds directly into NASIC. Traditional guardsmen at Springfield work for NASIC during the week and the base on weekends. The two installations function as a single intelligence apparatus distributed across two sites.

Tier 1 · Research Procurement

AFRL AMAC IDIQ — $10B Ceiling

Draft RFP issued December 18, 2025. Responses due January 9, 2026. Five-year base period plus three one-year options. Maximum dollar value of $10 billion across awarded vendors. The largest single research contract vehicle out of Wright-Patterson in recent memory. Forces a contractor expansion cycle through 2031.

AFMC spent nearly $1.3 billion with Dayton-area businesses last year alone. AFLCMC cleared roughly $83 billion in obligated contracts. The base economic footprint is already the largest single-site employment concentration in Ohio. AMAC adds programmatic durability on top of that and locks the demand pattern for the rest of the decade.

Tier 1 · Propulsion Industrial Base

GE Aerospace Hypersonic Test Infrastructure

Evendale · Hamilton County

June 2025: Evendale upgrades for dedicated test support of larger hypersonic propulsion systems at higher Mach numbers and mission-relevant scale. March 2025: $5 billion IDIQ for F110-GE-129 engines. Throughout 2025: ATLAS funding flowed through Title III of the Defense Production Act — the federal government's strongest signal that a corridor is being deliberately built into critical national infrastructure.

The Innoveering acquisition (2022) integrated specialist hypersonic propulsion expertise into the Evendale test platform. GE has demonstrated dual-mode ramjet concept-to-test in under 11 months. The development cadence itself is the signal — propulsion programs of this scale historically ran on 5 to 7 year cycles. Evendale is operating on a different timeline.

Why it matters

Cincinnati-Evendale is single-prime concentrated, structurally different from the Wright-Patt multi-vendor ecosystem. The thesis here is propulsion industrial base concentration, not corridor breadth.

No single Ohio anchor would justify the corridor thesis on its own. The four operating in parallel produce signal redundancy across every framework category that survives single-program risk.

Loxley Commercial · The Lattice Effect
The consolidation signal

The Dayton position institutional capital has already taken.

Among the public-record patterns that confirm the lattice thesis, one position deserves a separate line. CW Industrial Partners, a Cleveland-headquartered lower-middle-market PE firm with twenty-five years of industrial component consolidation experience, holds Libra Industries — a Dayton-headquartered electronics manufacturing and precision machining platform — at the exact Tier 2 supplier coordinates the Arsenal-1 production ramp will need at scale.

Phase 3 Validation Catalyst

CW Industrial Partners · Libra Industries

Dayton HQ · Cleveland sponsor · Tier 2 EMS & precision machining

The platform was assembled in sequence, not by accident. CW acquired Dayton's GEMCITY Engineering & Manufacturing in November 2017 — an 85-year-old tool-and-die heritage business serving aerospace, defense and industrial OEMs. Four years later CW merged GEMCITY into the Libra platform and made Dayton the corporate headquarters. As of January 2025 the Dayton site holds an ISO Class 7 cleanroom, forty-five CNC machines, recent capex on a Kitamura 5-axis vertical machining center and two Mitsubishi Wire EDM platforms tuned for thin-wall flexure tolerances. The capability stack reads as a sell-side preparation document.

The timing is structural. CW raised its first fund under the rebrand in January 2024. Lower-middle-market PE fund cycles produce portfolio mark-to-market events eighteen to thirty-six months later, which places Libra's sell-side window between mid-2026 and early 2027 — opening precisely as Arsenal-1 begins production and as Dayton's supplier densification becomes visible in contract awards and permit data.

Three acquirer archetypes. CW's prior aerospace and defense exits ran parallel processes across three channels — strategic primes (Ripley Tools to Hubbell), upstream PE consolidators (Avtron Aerospace to Odyssey, Capewell to Argosy) and tier-2 EMS roll-ups. The Libra process will rehearse the same playbook. Plausible names on the buyer surface include Huntington Ingalls Industries (already in Fairborn), L3Harris and Leonardo DRS Beavercreek for the prime path; Arcline Investment Management for the PE consolidator path (post-Novaria, $2.2B 2025); and Celestica, Benchmark Electronics or Sanmina for the EMS roll-up path.

Why it matters · Phase 3 validation

The premium-multiple sale of a Dayton aerospace components platform is the cleanest possible Phase 3 validation for the corridor. The first such transaction inside the lattice confirms institutional capital has re-rated the geography. Hospitality cap rate compression and ADR expansion historically follow within ninety to one hundred eighty days. The Huntsville pattern ran on the same template — local industrial PE assembled positions in defense-adjacent components in the early 2000s and sold into the post-BRAC cascade at premium multiples to East Coast primes, PE consolidators and tier-2 EMS roll-ups. The geography re-rated. The capital compounded. Dayton is running the same play.

Industrial Demand Cascade

Activation by stage.

The cascade moves in sequence: Jobs precede Hotels. Hotels precede Gathering Infrastructure. Gathering precedes Housing. Housing precedes Price Discovery. The timing window between each stage is the entry point for capital.

Jobs
Active · all four nodes
Anchor commitments and contractor expansions visible across all four sub-corridors. AFRL AMAC adds another five years of programmatic ramp on top of the current trajectory. Permanent hyperscale workforce forming in Pickaway County simultaneously with rotational TDY workforce continuing to grow at Wright-Patt.
Hotels
Miami Valley first · Pickaway behind
New flag construction in Beavercreek (dual-brand Marriott, Hampton + Homewood Suites). Existing extended-stay inventory reporting month-long stays as routine. Pickaway County is hospitality-underbuilt relative to Arsenal-1 ramp — the clearest near-term construction gap in the corridor.
Gathering
Wright-Patt mature · Pickaway emerging
Restaurant and retail activity continues to expand around the Wright State and Beavercreek office cluster. Pickaway County retail and gathering infrastructure has not yet adjusted to the workforce ramp visible in the Anduril hiring cadence. Coffee, lunch and convenience retail will follow within 12 to 18 months.
Housing
Multifamily window open
Beavercreek and Centerville school district enrollment is already showing the permanent-relocation pattern characteristic of mature defense corridors. Pickaway County multifamily is the asset class with the cleanest pre-cascade entry profile in the state. Hyperscale production sites pull permanent workforce ahead of the conventional cascade timing.
Price Discovery
Selective repricing in Beavercreek
Above-market rents are appearing in pockets of the Wright-Patt office submarket. Pickaway County land and submarket pricing has not yet repriced. The Miami Valley is partially priced. The Central Ohio defense submarket is not.
